Get Involved at the Lab!

Student Volunteer:

As a student volunteer you will:

  • Learn aquatic invertebrate sampling methods
  • Learn how to use a pH meter and YSI meter to measure dissolved oxygen and salinity
  • Learn how to use a dichotomous key to identify aquatic macroinvertebrates in the lab
  • Use a dissecting microscope to sort samples and find organisms
  • Learn about the importance of aquatic invertebrates in the environment
  • Spend 20 hours per quarter on the project for at least 2 quarters

A typical quarter consists of 8 weeks in the lab sorting organisms in the microscope and 2 weeks in the field collecting new samples. Students are also encouraged to read about the project and restoration effects and partake in data analysis. No experience needed to become a student volunteer, training will be provided from student leaders.

Read more about Kylie's experience as a student volunteer!

Student Intern:

As a student intern you will:

  • Do everything a student volunteer does
  • Earn academic credit (1-4 units)
  • Commit to at least 30 hours per quarter

For environmental studies majors there is a 30 hour per quarter commitment. This varies depending on your department. Reach out to your academic advisor for more information on applying for and earning academic credit through an internship.

Student Leader:

As a student leader you will:

  • Lead an independent research project (see projects tab for examples)
  • Work in a group to analyze your data- learn R studio if you are not yet familiar
  • Present your findings at a research symposium
  • Train new volunteers on lab and field work methods
  • Earn a small stipend for your time commitment
  • Spend 60 hours per quarter on the project

Typically at least one quarter of volunteering is required for the student leader position.

Please email Alison Rickard if you are interested in any of these opportunities or if you have a research project you are interested in pursuing.
